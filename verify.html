Business Case

Is going to the moon
easier than bank verification?

We can travel to the Moon in 72 hours today — but in Bangladesh, completing a Contact Point Verification (CPV) for a loan or credit card application takes just as long. CPV is how banks confirm an applicant's address, employment, and identity before approving financial products. Yet this critical process was stuck in an era of paper files, physical couriers, and manual data entry.

The numbers revealed a systemic crisis. Bangladesh's credit-to-GDP ratio stood at 45.22% against an ideal of 100 — meaning credit creation was being throttled not by lack of demand or supply, but by the sheer inefficiency of verification infrastructure.

Every week of delay in credit card or loan approval means money that isn't circulating, transactions that aren't happening, and businesses that aren't growing. The opportunity was clear: if we could compress verification time from 72 hours to same-day, we could meaningfully accelerate Bangladesh's financial inclusion.

Nearly 1 million hours per month are wasted collectively processing credit card and loan verifications manually across Bangladesh's banking sector — equivalent to convening multiple startup teams solely to move paperwork.

— Barikoi Primary Research / Medium, 2022
01
Painfully Slow TAT
Standard CPV took 48–72 hours at best. Manual routing of paperwork between branches, HQ, and field agents caused compounding delays at every handoff.
02
Fraud & Bribery Risk
Unmonitored field agents with no real-time tracking created opportunities for verification fraud and bribery — exposing banks to significant NPL risk.
03
Poor Geographic Coverage
Verification vendors had limited geographic reach. Applications from outside major cities had drastically longer timelines — sometimes weeks.
04
No Visibility or Audit Trail
Banks had no real-time view of where their applications stood. No location proof, no timestamp, no photo evidence — just a manual report filed after the fact.
05
22-Minute Manual Data Entry
Each application required a data entry operator to spend ~22 minutes manually inputting information from physical forms — a major throughput bottleneck.
Market Opportunity

Why this was the
right problem to solve.

Bangladesh's banking sector processes hundreds of thousands of CPV applications annually. A one-day verification cycle could double the loan sanctioning ratio, accelerate money circulation, and materially improve the country's credit-to-GDP ratio. The downstream impact of faster credit is faster business formation, more consumer spending, and higher GDP growth.

to build.

The PRD was organized around three product goals, each mapped to a North Star Metric that the team tracked weekly.

⏱
Speed
North Star: TAT < 48 hrs
Compress verification turnaround from the industry standard of 72+ hours to under 48 hours for Dhaka, same-day for priority files.
🔒
Trust & Compliance
North Star: 0 Fraud Incidents
Build a fully auditable, tamper-proof trail with GPS proof-of-visit, timestamped photos, and digital signatures for every verification.
⚡
Throughput
North Star: Files / Verifier / Day
Enable each verifier to process significantly more files per day than the manual model through intelligent geo-routing and task batching.

System Design

How Verify
actually works.

Verify operates on an Uber-model architecture: the bank creates a file, our algorithm segments the application location-wise, and the nearest available Verifier receives a trip notification on their mobile app — just like a driver accepting a ride. The entire process from file creation to bank submission is tracked, timestamped, and GPS-verified.

01
Bank Creates Application
Team Leader inputs applicant data, uploads photos (applicant, guarantor, documents). The system immediately geocodes the address using Barikoi's proprietary maps.
02
Algorithm Assigns Nearest Verifier
Barikoi's geographic segmentation engine identifies the nearest available Verifier in the correct district and sends them a push notification with trip details.
03
Verifier Completes & Submits
Verifier navigates, conducts the visit, fills the form, uploads geotagged photos + digital signature, and submits. The system auto-generates the bank submission PDF.
Phase 1 Team Leader — File Initiation
1.1
File Creation
Team Leader logs into the web dashboard and creates a new application file using information provided by the bank — including applicant name, NID, address, employment details, and loan/card type.
Actor: Team Leader · Platform: Web Dashboard
1.2
Image Upload & File Submission
Team Leader searches the file by name or ID, then uploads the applicant photo, guarantor photo, and the physical application file image. On submission, the system geocodes the address and queues the file for verifier assignment.
Actor: Team Leader · Trigger: Submission → Auto-geocoding → Trip Queue
↓
Phase 2 System — Intelligent Assignment
2.1
Geographic Segmentation & Verifier Matching
Barikoi's algorithm analyzes the file's location coordinates and segments it to the correct operational zone. The nearest available Verifier covering that zone is identified and sent a push notification.
Actor: System · Technology: Barikoi Maps API + Geo-segmentation Engine
↓
Phase 3 Verifier — Field Verification
3.1
Accept Trip
Verifier receives the push notification on the mobile app, reviews the trip details (address, applicant info, file type), and accepts the assignment. Verifier can see all pending trips plotted on a map.
Actor: Verifier · Platform: iOS / Android App
3.2
Navigate & Plan Route
Verifier uses the in-app map to navigate to the contact point. If multiple trips are accepted, the map shows all pins enabling efficient route planning to batch visits geographically.
Actor: Verifier · Technology: Barikoi Maps Navigation
3.3
Conduct Visit & Fill Verification Form
At the contact point, Verifier fills in all required verification fields — address confirmation, employment status, business status, income estimate, and any discrepancies from the application.
Actor: Verifier · Validation: Required fields enforced; form locked until GPS within 200m of target address
3.4
Upload Proof-of-Visit Evidence
Verifier captures geotagged, timestamped photos of the address and contact point. Then uploads their digital signature and the Letter of Introduction (LOI) image. All evidence is immutably stored with GPS coordinates.
Actor: Verifier · Evidence: Photos + GPS stamp + Timestamp + Digital Signature + LOI
↓
Phase 4 Team Leader — Monitoring & QA
4.1
Real-Time Verifier Location Tracking
Team Leader monitors all active Verifiers' live GPS positions on the dashboard map. Geo-fence alerts flag any Verifier who submits a form without being physically present at the target address — eliminating remote fraud.
Actor: Team Leader · Feature: Live map + Geo-fence enforcement
4.2
Progress Dashboard & Analytics
Team Leader tracks the overall pipeline — files pending, in-progress, and completed — with analytics on daily throughput, verifier performance, and district-level completion rates. Used to identify bottlenecks and rebalance workloads.
Actor: Team Leader · Metrics: Files / verifier / day · TAT per district · Completion rate
↓
Phase 5 Team Leader — Bank Submission
5.1
Auto-Generated PDF Download & Submission
Once verified, the system auto-generates a bank-formatted submission PDF containing the applicant/guarantor photos, all form data, verifier details, GPS proof-of-visit, timestamped photos, and the digital LOI. Team Leader downloads, prints if required, and submits to the bank — no manual compilation needed.
Actor: Team Leader · Output: Bank-formatted PDF with complete audit trail

PM Reflection

What I learned
building Verify.

The hardest product problems are organizational, not technical. The biggest challenge wasn't building the geo-routing algorithm — it was convincing bank operations teams to trust a mobile app over their paper-based muscle memory. Change management was as much the product as the software.

Fraud prevention must be designed in, not bolted on. The geo-fence enforcement and in-app-only photo capture features weren't nice-to-haves — they were the reason banks renewed contracts. Trust is a feature.

North Star Metrics keep teams honest at scale. When we were shipping 53 features in one quarter, it was easy for the team to celebrate velocity. Our NSM (files per verifier per day) kept us anchored to outcomes, not output.

Delivery density is its own moat. As more verifiers joined and more districts were covered, the cost per verification fell and quality improved. This flywheel — more agents → more coverage → more clients → more volume → more agents — became the durable competitive advantage.

The PRD must survive first contact with reality. Our original roadmap had E-Verify (AI self-service) as P1. After talking to bank compliance officers, we learned no bank would accept AI-only verification for credit decisions in 2023. We reclassified it as P2 and prioritized trust-building features first. Always validate priority with the actual decision-maker.
